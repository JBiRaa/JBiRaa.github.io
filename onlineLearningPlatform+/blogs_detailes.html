What is success?

If you've been struggling to define the meaning of a successful life, we have two good news for you: One: It's never too late to start over. Two: You have to write your own definition of success. Many of us go after professional titles, money, or social status—and yet we don't feel successful when we get those things. This is because you can only measure success in your life when you determine what makes you happy. Success is something you have to define for yourself, and no one can do it for you. Success can mean giving back to the world and making a difference. It may mean a sense of accomplishment and career advancement. It could mean being able to do the things you love. This may mean being able to provide the healthiest possible environment for your children. It is entirely up to you. It is impossible to take a one-size-fits-all approach. You have to believe that what you do matters. We need to make sure that our work affects the lives of others. You may know that when we think we are engaged in meaningful work, we feel more responsible, and more prepared to show up. People who feel that their work is beneficial are more likely to stay in their job. Regardless of your type of job, career success is the ability to feel positive with your career choices and the results of those choices. Tell us, what is success for you?

External measures of success

External measures of success are goals that can be tested, evaluated, and monitored. It can be either by others or by yourself. They are tools like prestige, awards, and certificates. The external measures of success can vary from one to the other as long as it makes you happy. You just need to be careful in choosing the right goals that express yourself; This is because judging your career by these metrics alone can affect your self-esteem.

internal success metrics

Internal measures of success are subjective matters that only you can sense. What we mean by that is that every action you take, or a goal you pursue, will eventually come back to you. No matter what happens, your decision is the measure. Example: Believing that you make a difference in your environment, if you have a job, or any of the other things, is an internal measure of success for you. You should be grateful for all of these metrics.

sustainable career

While we talk a lot about finding our passion, we realize that there are some limitations. Sometimes our passion for certain things can change, and sometimes the things we enjoy doing aren't necessarily the ones that give us the most promising opportunities in life. Instead of focusing on a fixed position or job, perhaps we should think of something that improves our mentality about and thinking about things. “A sustainable career is a dynamic and flexible process; it is characterized by continuous learning, constant renewal, security that comes from employability, and a harmonious fit with your skills, interests and values. The keys to having a sustainable career are knowing yourself – what interests you? What do you do well? Better and not in a good way?What motivates you? A sustainable career is one that matches our values, provides us with the financial security we need, and takes care of our needs. Through this, we can be flexible enough to change the stages of our lives; Who we can rely on and adapt to our interests. It gives us the feeling that our family time and personal life matter to us. It gives us the freedom to make the changes we need, whether we want to take another career path. It's all in adapting to the rapid change in the labor market.

happy healthy life

So far we have discussed measuring career success and achieving a meaningful life. Now, we'll look more at how to measure the success of a healthy, happy life. Researchers have found that people who take time off from work are more satisfied with their work. They can focus more on their jobs and be able to make better decisions. The process of working overtime and not taking time off puts our lives at great risk. It is not good for ourselves or the organization we work for. We need to take care of our health and give more time to spend with family. We need to take time off so we don't affect our productivity or make more mistakes.
